Temperature effect on velocity

Sound velocity changes with temperature in all materials. This effect is much greater in plastics and rubber than in metals or ceramics.

Metals / Ceramics
Small change with temperature. Standard calibration usually sufficient.
Plastics / Rubber
Rapid velocity change. Always recalibrate at actual test material temperature.
⚠️ TEMPERATURE LIMIT: Standard contact transducers are damaged above ~50 C (125 F). Use delay line, immersion, or high-temperature dual element transducers.

Modes of measurement - Transducer types

Reference
Modes of measurement
1
Mode 1 - Pulse to first back wall echo
Measures time between the excitation pulse and the first back wall echo, then subtracts a zero offset. The most common mode, used with contact transducers.
Best max thickness Simple setup Castings and rubber Higher min thickness Max 50C surface
2
Mode 2 - Interface echo to back wall echo
Measures time between the interface echo (front surface) and the first back wall echo. Requires delay line or immersion transducers.
Plastics and composites High temperatures Curved surfaces In-line measurement Max thickness limited
3
Mode 3 - Between two successive back wall echoes
Measures time between two successive back wall echoes representing one complete round trip. Highest accuracy and best minimum thickness resolution. Removes coatings from measurement.
Highest accuracy Best min resolution Removes coatings Requires clean echoes Low attenuation only
DE
Dual Element - Corrosion survey mode
Separate transmitting and receiving elements in a V-path. Optimized for rough, corroded surfaces and pitting detection. More rugged, less precise than single-element for precision gauging.
Corroded surfaces High temperatures Pitting detection Less precise Not for precision gauging
Transducer types
🔵
Contact
Direct contact with the test piece. Hard wearplate protects the element. Simplest to implement.
Use for: most metals, ceramics, standard gauging. Surface must be below 50 C.
📶
Delay Line
Plastic/epoxy cylinder between element and test piece. Separates excitation pulse from thin-material echoes. Thermal insulator.
Use for: thin materials, high temperatures, curved or contoured surfaces.
💧
Immersion
Water column or bath couples sound to the test piece. No direct contact. Can be focused for sharp radiuses.
Use for: in-line production, scanning, sharp curves, grooves, high precision.
🔴
Dual Element
Separate transmit and receive elements on a delay line. V-path creates pseudo-focusing for minimum remaining wall thickness.
Use for: corrosion surveys, rough surfaces, pitting, high-temperature inspection.
